The Visayas is one of the three main geographical divisions of the Philippines, alongside Luzon and Mindanao. It comprises several islands in the central part of the archipelago, including the major islands of Panay, Negros, Cebu, Bohol, Leyte, and Samar, among others. The Visayas region is known for its rich cultural heritage, stunning beaches, and vibrant festivals. It has played a significant role in Philippine history, being the site of early Spanish colonization, as well as important events during the Philippine Revolution against Spanish rule and World War II. Today, the Visayas is a diverse and dynamic region, contributing to the country's economy through agriculture, tourism, and industry.

Flavors of Visayas

Chicken Inasal
Originating from Bacolod City in Negros Occidental, inasal is a type of grilled chicken marinated in a mixture of vinegar, calamansi (Philippine lime), garlic, lemongrass, and annatto oil. It's often served with a side of garlic rice and a dipping sauce made with vinegar, soy sauce, and chili.
Batchoy
Hailing from the city of Iloilo in Panay Island, batchoy is a comforting noodle soup made with pork offal, beef loin, and egg noodles, garnished with crispy garlic, spring onions, and sometimes chicharron (pork cracklings). It's known for its rich, savory broth.
Dried Mangoes
Cebu, in particular, is famous for its dried mangoes, which are sweet, tangy, and chewy. These dried fruits are a popular snack both locally and internationally, showcasing the delicious flavors of Philippine mangoes.
Sinamak
Sinamak is a spicy vinegar condiment commonly found in the Visayas region, particularly in places like Iloilo and Antique. It's made by infusing coconut vinegar with garlic, ginger, shallots, chili peppers, and sometimes other herbs and spices. Sinamak adds a flavorful kick to various dishes and is often used as a dipping sauce for grilled meats or as a condiment for seafood.
Piaya
Piaya is a popular Filipino delicacy originating from the province of Negros Occidental in the Visayas region of the Philippines. It is a sweet flatbread made from unleavened dough that is filled with muscovado sugar (a type of unrefined cane sugar) and sometimes flavored with sesame seeds. The dough is flattened into thin discs, then the sugar filling is added before it is folded and sealed, forming a flatbread.



Travel Destinations in Visayas

Boracay Island
Known for its powdery white sand beaches and vibrant nightlife, Boracay is one of the most popular tourist destinations in the Philippines. Visitors can enjoy activities such as swimming, snorkeling, diving, and island.
Siquijor Island
Known as the "Island of Fire," Siquijor is shrouded in mystery and folklore. Visitors can explore pristine beaches, hidden waterfalls, and centuries-old churches, as well as experience traditional healings and rituals.
Chocolate Hills
A geological formation consisting of more than 1,000 hills covered in grass that turns brown during the dry season, the Chocolate Hills is one of the most iconic landmarks in the Philippines. Visitors can enjoy panoramic views from viewing decks and explore nearby attractions such as the Tarsier Conservation Area and Loboc River.
The Ruins
One of the most iconic landmarks in Bacolod, The Ruins is a mansion built in the early 20th century by the sugar baron Don Mariano Ledesma Lacson. Despite being burned down during World War II, its structure still stands proudly amidst beautifully landscaped gardens, making it a popular spot for photography and sunset viewing.



Visayan Experience

Mountain Trekking and Hiking
he Visayas offer stunning landscapes for mountain trekking and hiking. Places like Osmeña Peak in Cebu or Kanlaon Volcano in Negros provide adventurous trails with breathtaking views.
Beach and Island Hopping
With its pristine beaches and crystal-clear waters, Visayas is a paradise for beach lovers. Boracay, often called the "White Beach," is a popular destination. Island hopping around Palawan and Guimaras is also a must.
Water Sports
The Visayas are a haven for water sports enthusiasts. Whether it's diving in Apo Island, snorkeling in Moalboal, or trying out kiteboarding in Boracay, there's no shortage of aquatic adventures.
Mascara Festival
Bacolod, in Negros Occidental, hosts the Mascara Festival, known for its unique mask-making and vibrant street dance competitions. The festival is a lively showcase of creativity and local culture.
Sinulog Festival
Held in Cebu City, the Sinulog Festival is a vibrant celebration honoring the Santo Niño. It features a grand parade with colorful costumes, lively street dancing, and a religious procession.
